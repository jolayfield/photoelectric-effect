Theory

The Photoelectric Effect occurs when light hits a material and ejects electrons. Classically, light wave intensity should drive ejection. However, quantum mechanics shows light acts as particles (photons).

$$ K_{max} = h\nu - \Phi $$

Electrons are only ejected if the photon energy (\(h\nu\)) exceeds the metal's Work Function (\(\Phi\)).
